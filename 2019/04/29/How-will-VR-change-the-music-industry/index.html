How will VR change the music industry?

Posted on 2019-04-29   |   In Technology   |  

Have you ever been to the live concert of your favorite singer? How was your experience then? For myself, although I was very excited when I went to Jay Chou’s concert in 2017, the experience was not as good as expected. Because there were tens of thousands of people and my seat was far away from the stage, I could hardly see Jay Chou and spent most of the time looking at the big screen. However, if we can use Virtual Reality (VR) technology in the music industry, concerts and music videos will be subverted, and we can bring our customers a far better experience than the present one.

When we think about the customer of the music industry, it would be almost everyone. We listen to music when we drive to work, exercise in the gym, or just stay in bed and rest. In other words, almost everyone listens to music every day. However, in the past ten years, the form of live concert and music video do not change a lot. If we want to go to our idols’ show, we need to search for the concert information, wait for the ticket competition, and if lucky, we can finally go to the concert after several months. For music videos, maybe we cannot distinguish the one at present and the one in the past. It would be so beautiful for us if we can join a music concert at home but also have an experience like a real concert. VR can make this dream possible.

VR is an interactive computer-generated experience taking place within a simulated environment. It incorporates mainly auditory and visual feedback, which is very suitable for the music industry. Current VR technology most commonly uses virtual reality headsets, sometimes in combination with physical environments or props, to generate realistic images, sounds and other sensations that simulate a user’s physical presence in a virtual environment. If we use VR technology to record a music concert and use it to make the live music video, we can feel like we are actually at a concert and sit really close to the singer. In this kind of music video, we are able to “look around” the artificial world, move around in it, and even interact the virtual items. What’s more, this kind of concert can get rid of the limit of space, which means a music concert does not need to be held in a stadium or a particular place, it can be held almost everywhere. I am pretty optimistic about the future market of it.

Coldplay’s Virtual Reality Concert

Maybe you would ask if this kind of live music video is so nice, why don’t we see it in the market? Actually, some famous singing stars tried to use VR technology in their concert. My idol, Faye Wong, had a VR music concert called Faye’s Moments in 2016 and it was quite different from the traditional music concert. It was recorded and transformed to live VR streaming video. There were 88 thousand people who watched the live VR concert. Coldplay is a famous British rock band which is renowned for its live concert. They also tried live show in VR on August 17 in Chicago. However, they are more like experiments and do not reach a wide range of customers. The reason behind that is partly because nowadays the cost of recording and making a live VR concert is prohibitive. Moreover, customers who have VR devices are not so much. If we do not have any VR device, it is hard to have a taste of the benefit to the live VR music concert.

With the development of VR technology, the expense of making a VR video will become much cheaper than today. What’s more, it is also possible that in five years, VR/AR features will be a basic standard for a smartphone. We may still need an additional VR device, but the VR device may become a necessary accessory for the smartphone. By then, the market for live VR music video will grow very fast. The live VR music video will bring a brand new experience for us and change the music concert and music video industry fundamentally. As a music lover, I could not wait for this day.

Thank you for your reading and see you next time.
